MARTIAN ATMOSPHERE

The composition of the Martian atmosphere was determined in the mid 1970s by the Viking landers. Carbon dioxide is the principal constituent, followed by nitrogen, argon, oxygen, and carbon monoxide. Trace amounts of the noble gases are also present. Additional minor and highly variable constituents include water vapor, ozone, and dust particles.
